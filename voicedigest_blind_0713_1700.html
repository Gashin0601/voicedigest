Why these three fixes matter for fully blind users

AmbiguityFixed — no silent guess. A dedicated role="group" card states the uncertainty in words, gives two real buttons ("Yes, add it" / "Skip it"), and a native <details> to hear the raw line before deciding. A screen reader lands on this and immediately knows a decision is being asked of it — nothing is implied by layout alone.
Information overloadFixed — the card leads with one sentence (the headline) and a one-line context sub-line. Everything else — key points, citations, audio, transcript, feedback — sits inside a native <details>/<summary> labeled "Hear more". Screen readers announce it as a collapsed/expanded toggle automatically; a sighted skim and an ear-first skim now take the same first step.
Notification overloadFixed — a persistent status strip states the current interrupt threshold in plain language ("only urgent items will interrupt you") instead of leaving it invisible. A disclosure explains exactly what qualifies, so the person can calibrate trust in the threshold instead of being surprised by silence or by pings.
